Rahul Biswas

Postdoctoral Researcher · Department of Neurology · University of California, San Francisco

Department of Neurology

University of California, San Francisco

San Francisco, CA

rahul.biswas@ucsf.edu

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Neurology at the University of California, San Francisco, working with Dr. Reza Abbasi-Asl. My research sits at the intersection of computational neuroscience, causal inference, and AI-driven digital health — developing principled statistical and machine learning methods that can uncover causal structure in complex biological signals and translate toward real-world clinical impact.

Computational Neuroscience. I develop nonparametric causal inference frameworks for high-resolution neural time series. Current projects include:

  • Revealing multi-scale directed network dynamics of chronic pain from EEG and ECoG recordings (with Dr. Prasad Shirvalkar, UCSF Pain Study)
  • Mapping visual cortex circuitry reorganization under varying arousal states in mice (calcium imaging & Neuropixels data)
  • AI-based reconstruction of visual experience from fMRI using biologically-constrained generative video models (with Dr. Jack Gallant, UC Berkeley)

AI Digital Health. I co-lead the development of AI-assisted remote diagnostic tools:

  • Home-based cardiovascular diagnostics — blood pressure assessment from wearable sensors, including a provisional US patent (with Dr. Julio Lamprea, UCSF Cardiovascular Prevention Center)
  • Skin lesion diagnostics — robust remote assessment from smartphone images (with Dr. Maria Wei, UCSF Dermatology & SF VA)

Previously, I completed my PhD in Electrical and Computer Engineering (with a secondary MS in Statistics) at the University of Washington, advised by Dr. Eli Shlizerman, where I developed causal discovery algorithms for neural time series and mapped directional functional connectivity in Alzheimer’s disease. I hold a BS and MS in Statistics from the Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata.

I am on the academic job market for faculty positions. Feel free to reach out!

selected publications

  1. Nat Commun
    CITS: Nonparametric Statistical Causal Modeling for High-Resolution Neural Time Series
    Rahul Biswas, Srikanth Sripada, Subhabrata Mukherjee, and 1 more author
    Nature Communications, 2025
    In Review
    Abs arXiv

    We introduce CITS, a nonparametric statistical framework for causal inference in high-resolution neural time series, enabling discovery of neural functional circuitry dynamics.

  2. Front. Comput. Neurosci.
    Causal Functional Connectivity in Alzheimer’s Disease Computed from Time Series fMRI Data
    Rahul Biswas and Srikanth Sripada
    Frontiers in Computational Neuroscience, 2023
    Abs DOI

    We apply causal inference methods to fMRI time series to map directional functional connectivity differences in Alzheimer’s disease, Mild Cognitive Impairment, and Cognitively Normal subjects.

  3. Stat. Comput.
    Consistent Causal Inference from Time Series with PC Algorithm and its Time-Aware Extension
    Rahul Biswas and Subhabrata Mukherjee
    Statistics and Computing, 2023
    Abs

    We establish consistency guarantees for the PC algorithm applied to time series data and introduce the Time-Aware PC (TPC) extension for improved causal discovery in temporal settings.

  4. PLoS Comput. Biol.
    Statistical Perspective on Functional and Causal Neural Connectomics: The Time-Aware PC Algorithm
    Rahul Biswas and Eli Shlizerman
    PLOS Computational Biology, 2022
    Abs DOI

    We introduce the Time-Aware PC (TPC) algorithm, a principled causal discovery method for neural time series, and apply it to characterize directed functional connectivity in neural circuits.
