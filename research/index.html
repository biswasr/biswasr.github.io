Research

My research develops principled statistical and machine learning methods to uncover causal structure in biological signals, with applications spanning systems neuroscience, clinical neurology, and AI-driven digital health.

Computational Neuroscience

Causal Inference in Neural Time Series

Nonparametric statistical frameworks for discovering causal structure in high-resolution neural recordings

Network Dynamics of Chronic Pain

Uncovering directed brain network signatures of chronic pain from human EEG and ECoG recordings

Visual Cortex Circuitry and Arousal

Characterizing state-dependent organization of microscale functional circuitry in visual cortex using calcium imaging and Neuropixels

Reconstructing Visual Experience from Brain Activity

AI-based decoding of visual experience from fMRI using biologically-constrained generative video models

AI Digital Health

AI-Assisted Home Cardiovascular Diagnostics

Machine learning algorithms for home-based blood pressure assessment from wearable sensors

AI-Assisted Skin Cancer Screening and Diagnostics

AI and machine learning methods for earlier and more accurate diagnosis of skin cancers and disorders from smartphone images
